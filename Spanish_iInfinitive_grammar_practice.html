Advanced Spanish: Infinitive Structures

Instructions:

  • Translate, complete, transform, or correct the sentences as instructed. Use infinitive structures wherever appropriate.
  • Negative subjunctive/imperative forms (e.g., No olvides...) are included. For commands, use the correct negative imperative.
  • For translation, multiple correct answers are possible (especially for verbs like to try: intentar, tratar de, procurar, esforzarse por).
  • Full stops at the end are ignored.
  • Use hints if you get stuck. If your answer is not accepted, check with your teacher or a native speaker-it may still be correct!
  • Press Enter to check your answer. Model answers will appear after several attempts.

Grammar: Many verbs are followed directly by an infinitive (e.g., deber, tener que, hay que, poder, querer, soler, necesitar, saber, intentar, decidir, esperar, conseguir, mandar, olvidar, parecer). Some, like tratar de or esforzarse por, use a preposition before the infinitive. For negative commands (imperatives), use the present subjunctive. Pronouns can often go before the conjugated verb or attached to the infinitive (e.g., Quiero verla / La quiero ver).
