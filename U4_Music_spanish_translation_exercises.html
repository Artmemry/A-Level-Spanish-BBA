Exercise 1: Translation into English - El éxito de El canto del loco

Spanish Text (10 marks):

Este fin de semana el grupo El canto del loco actuará en la plaza de toros de Las Ventas. El Canto del loco se convirtió en el símbolo de la vanguardia madrileña, y en el tiempo que lleva actuando ha batido todos los récords de ventas. Empezará su gira en julio y tocará unos treinta conciertos en diferentes provincias españolas. A los componentes del grupo les gusta presentar una imagen informal y que muestre que no les interesa la fama.

Exercise 2: Translation into Spanish (Easy) - Flamenco Music

English Text (20 marks):

Flamenco is considered the most representative and typical genre of music and dance in Spain, and many foreigners think of flamenco when they are asked about their idea of Spain. Its origins go back centuries, and it is undeniable that the rhythm and sounds have been influenced by Arabic music. Many people are passionate about flamenco artists such as Paco de Lucía, but flamenco continues to be a music genre that not everyone in Spain can understand.

Exercise 3: Translation into Spanish (Medium) - Dancing in the Hispanic World

English Text (30 marks):

Both in Spain and in Latin America, for centuries dancing has been a representation of the culture and the identity of its inhabitants. From the lascivious and sensual tango in Argentina, to the sounds of the bagpipes in northern Spain, music and dancing seem to be intrinsically linked to everyday life in Hispanic countries. Many people are interested in visiting cities in Spain at festival time in order to be able to admire not only the dancers' ability, but also the spectacular dancing costumes. Some regional dances, such as salsa in Cuba, have become the emblem of the country.

Exercise 4: Translation into Spanish (Hard) - Modern Idols

English Text (40 marks):

These days, for many teenagers, young people and even adults, singers and bands have become role models to imitate and admire. Although many maintain that music will never be the same since the arrival of programmes like X-Factor or The Voice, others argue that not only will there always be talent but this talent will be a source of admiration and respect. Never have celebrities had such a multitude of followers who watch, analyse and judge their every move. It is unquestionable that some of these celebrities have become real legends, but critics wonder whether being able to sing a song should be reason enough for their success. Future generations will decide whether their fame was deserved and will last forever.
